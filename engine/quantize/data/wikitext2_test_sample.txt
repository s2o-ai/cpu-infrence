 = Tropical Storm Alex ( 2010 ) =

 Tropical Storm Alex was the first named storm of the 2010 Atlantic hurricane season . Alex developed from a tropical wave that moved off the coast of Africa on June 19 . The system moved westward across the Atlantic and Caribbean Sea , gradually organizing . On June 25 , the system became a tropical depression while located south of Jamaica . It intensified into Tropical Storm Alex on June 26 and continued to strengthen as it moved into the Gulf of Mexico .

 = = Meteorological history = =

 A tropical wave departed the coast of Africa on June 19 and moved westward across the Atlantic Ocean . The wave showed little organization initially but began to develop convection as it approached the Caribbean Sea . By June 24 , the system had become sufficiently organized to be classified as a tropical disturbance . The National Hurricane Center began monitoring the system for potential tropical cyclone development .

 On June 25 , the disturbance developed a closed low @-@ level circulation and was classified as Tropical Depression One . The depression was located approximately 200 miles south of Jamaica at the time of classification . Moving northwestward , the system continued to organize and intensified into Tropical Storm Alex on June 26 . Alex reached its initial peak intensity with maximum sustained winds of 65 mph on June 27 , while located in the central Gulf of Mexico .

 = = Preparations = =

 As Alex approached the coast of Mexico , hurricane warnings were issued for portions of the Mexican coast from Tampico to La Cruz . Tropical storm warnings were also issued for parts of the Texas coast . Evacuations were ordered in low @-@ lying coastal areas of northeastern Mexico . Oil companies began evacuating workers from offshore platforms in the Gulf of Mexico .

 = = Impact = =

 Alex made landfall near Soto la Marina , Tamaulipas , Mexico , on June 30 with maximum sustained winds of 110 mph , making it a Category 2 hurricane on the Saffir @-@ Simpson Hurricane Wind Scale . The storm brought heavy rainfall and flooding to northeastern Mexico and southern Texas . Total rainfall amounts exceeded 12 inches in some areas . The flooding caused significant damage to infrastructure and agriculture in the affected regions .

Anarchism calls for the abolition of the state , which it holds to be unnecessary , undesirable , and harmful .
